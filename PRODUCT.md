# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Firmware and embedded-systems recruiters and hiring managers — people scanning
quickly for technical depth (MCUs, RTOS, BLE/nRF Connect SDK, C/C++, hardware
bring-up) before deciding whether to shortlist. They arrive skeptical and
time-pressed, often from a LinkedIn link or a resume, frequently on mobile.
Secondary: engineering peers and collaborators who land here from a project or
profile link.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Truong Minh Triet, a biomedical/firmware engineer. It
exists to convert a cold visitor into a warm lead: prove genuine engineering
credibility, make the project work explorable, surface the resume/CV, and make
contact effortless. Success = a recruiter leaves confident in the skills, with
the resume downloaded or a message sent. No single CTA dominates; the page must
serve credibility, project depth, resume download, and contact equally well.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, technical, and quietly confident — an engineer who ships, not a showman.
Three words: **precise, kinetic, grounded**. The voice is competent and direct,
never boastful. The aesthetic is **Cyberpunk 2077 / Edgerunners "Night City
clean UI"**: the in-game menu/HUD language — acid-yellow on near-black, sharp
angular cut-corner panels, monospace data readouts, restrained and highly
readable rather than glitch-drenched. The cyberpunk reading should come from
structure, type, and color discipline, not from decoration. It should feel like
a piece of well-engineered hardware: every element has a function.

## Anti-references

- **Generic corporate/SaaS portfolios.** Blue-and-white, rounded cards, Inter,
  centered hero with a gradient blob. The safe template look. Avoid entirely.
- **Costume cyberpunk.** Matrix digital rain, decorative katakana used purely as
  texture, cheesy "h4ck3r" tropes, neon everything with no hierarchy. Cyberpunk
  must be earned through real UI structure, not pasted-on clichés.
- **Overdone glitch.** Constant chromatic aberration / scanline noise that hurts
  readability. Glitch is a rare accent, not the baseline.
- **Default LinkedIn-style résumé page.** A flat chronological wall of text with
  no point of view.

## Design Principles

- **Looks like hardware, reads like a spec sheet.** Every panel, label, and
  readout should feel purposeful and engineered — function first, decoration
  earned. This is the credibility argument made visually.
- **Restraint is the cyberpunk.** Night City's UI is powerful because it's
  disciplined: one acid accent, sharp geometry, generous black. Resist the urge
  to add more neon; subtraction is the move.
- **Show the work, don't claim it.** Lead with concrete projects, certifications,
  and stack depth over adjectives. Recruiters trust evidence.
- **Fast and legible under pressure.** A time-pressed recruiter on mobile must
  parse it instantly. Performance, contrast, and clear hierarchy outrank any
  effect that fights them.
- **Frictionless conversion.** Resume download and contact are always within
  reach; never make the visitor hunt for the next step.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1. Acid-yellow
  on near-black passes comfortably; verify any yellow-on-mid-tone or
  muted-on-dark combinations and bump toward the ink end if close.
- **Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support** is required: all glitch, scanline,
  flicker, and entrance motion must have a static or crossfade fallback.
- Keyboard-navigable, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, and meaningful
  alt text on project/certification imagery.
- Don't encode meaning in color alone (color-blind safe): pair the acid accent
  with shape, label, or position.
